About Me

My name is Kevin Kato, and I am a third-year Data Science and Finance student at Northeastern University. I am passionate about creating data-driven insights and using information to solve real problems. What excites me most about data is that it can tell a story. Whether I am analyzing a business problem, building a predictive model, or organizing information into a dashboard, I enjoy finding patterns that help people make better decisions. I am especially interested in the way data can connect technical skills with practical outcomes in business, sports, and operations.

I have had the opportunity to work in several different environments that helped shape my interests. At Mukai Craft Brewing, I gained customer service and operations experience in a small business setting. At Foundation Medicine, I applied machine learning techniques to analyze how internal factors influenced customer attrition. At Wayfair, I worked on root cause analysis and helped create an actionable dashboard to track the performance of warehouses and transit routes. These experiences taught me how important it is to combine analytical thinking with communication and teamwork.

Outside of school and work, I enjoy sports, movies, TV shows, and music. I have also lived in five different states and in two countries outside of the United States, which helped me appreciate different cultures and perspectives. I enjoy traveling, meeting new people, and trying new things. Some of the projects I have worked on include a baseball prediction model, a Champions League match predictor, a fake news detection tool, and a health tracking app. This website highlights one of my biggest interests: baseball, especially with the 2026 World Baseball Classic creating so much excitement for fans around the world.
